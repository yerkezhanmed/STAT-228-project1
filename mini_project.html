Netflix’s dataset includes thousands of titles, ranging from blockbuster movies to binge-worthy TV series. Let’s see how the number of releases has changed over time.

** Are We Getting More Netflix Content Than Ever?🤔⁉️**

ggplot(netflix_clean, aes(x = release_year)) +
  geom_bar(fill = "red", alpha = 0.8) +
  labs(
    title = "Netflix Content Explosion: Titles Released Over the Years",
    x = "Release Year",
    y = "Number of Titles"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()

📢 🔅Key Insights

  • Is Netflix producing more content than ever before? Yes✅ Netflix has significantly increased its content production over time.

The first chart shows the number of Netflix titles released each year.

Prior to 2000, there were very few releases, but after 2010, the number of titles grew rapidly.

The peak appears around 2018-2020, suggesting that Netflix invested heavily in original content during this period - What trends stand out? From 2000 to 2010, Netflix started increasing its library but at a slow pace. A small fall is visible post-2020, which may be due to pandemic-related production delays or a shift in content strategy.

🎭 What Are the Most Popular Netflix Genres?

netflix_genres <- netflix_clean %>%
  separate_rows(listed_in, sep = ", ") %>%
  count(listed_in, sort = TRUE)

ggplot(head(netflix_genres, 10), aes(y = reorder(listed_in, n), x = n)) +
  geom_bar(stat = "identity", fill = "black", alpha = 0.8) +
  labs(
    title = "Top 10 Most Common Netflix Genres",
    x = "Number of Titles",
    y = "Genre"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()

📢 Key Insights

  • Which genres dominate Netflix? This chart chart displays the top 10 most common Netflix genres. International content is leading, showing Netflix’s global expansion strategy.Then it goes Dramas & Comedies, indicating their popularity across diverse audiences.

  • Are some genres growing more than others? Documentaries & Action/Adventure genres are also prominent, showing Netflix’s expansion beyond scripted dramas. Romantic Movies & Children’s content appear in the lower half, suggesting that these genres are less dominant in the Netflix catalog. —
